About Arbor MUN

The Social Science Department, with student organizers, proudly presents our school's Model United Nations (MUN) conference.

What is a Model United Nations (MUN)?

A Model United Nations (MUN) simulates global bodies, where students become diplomats or characters. They research, debate, and resolve pressing issues through negotiation and diplomacy.

Why Participate?

MUN develops critical thinking, public speaking, and leadership skills while fostering understanding of international relations and diplomacy.

Academic Development

Enhances understanding of political science, international relations, and global history while encouraging critical thinking.

Skill Building

Develops public speaking, debating and leadership skills while boosting confidence and research capabilities.

Character Growth

Instils empathy by encouraging understanding of diverse perspectives and promotes collaboration.

Our Committees & Agendas

Explore our diverse committees that cater to various interests and skill levels.

POLITICAL
Single Delegation

Lok Sabha

Simulating the Indian Parliament, delegates act as Members of Parliament, debating national issues, reforms, and lawmaking within India's democratic context.

Agenda:

Addressing the misuse of freedom of speech, with emphasis on the politicization of religion amidst communal tensions and terrorism in India.

CRISIS
Double Delegation

UNGA-ESS

A dynamic, fast-paced crisis simulation. Delegates represent defence, intelligence, and administrative authorities, responding in real-time to evolving crises.

Agenda:

Deliberation on the Situation in the Middle East Following the Suez Canal Nationalization, Freeze Date: 1 November 1956

FANTASY
Double Delegation

The Wizarding Council

Set in J.K. Rowling's magical world, this imaginative committee lets students role-play characters like Dumbledore or Death Eaters. Delegates debate magical law, ministry reforms, and dark forces.

Agenda:

Regulation of Dark magic and Unforgivable Curses in the Post-Voldemort era.

Your Facilitators

Meet the team that makes Arbor MUN 2025 possible.

Executive Board (EB)

Chair

The Chair leads committee debates, ensuring rules are followed, discussions stay focused, and guiding delegates through resolution writing. They are the main facilitator.

Vice-Chair

The Vice-Chair assists the Chair, handling procedural matters, managing speaking times, and ensuring committee decorum. They are the Chair's primary support.

International Press

The International Press acts as conference media, observing debates, interviewing delegates, and fact-checking points to add realism and external perspective.

Rapporteur

The Rapporteur accurately documents committee progress, noting speeches, resolutions, and voting results. Their role is to provide a clear record and summary of all sessions.
